Hello I'm James Tolliver, but I go by Wesley!

I'm a systems programmer with a wide range of skills from web dev to database administration to simple game dev. I have a passion for programming and I'm always seeking new ways to develop practical and extendable software.

Ever since I was first exposed to programming in my middle school lego robotics club, I've been irresistably drawn to it. Throughout high school/early college I took multiple programming classes as electives but didn't think I would ever be able to program professionally. When I signed up for university I picked Environmental Science as my major. I didn't really know what I wanted to do but was interested in learning some science - so I chose one I thought would give me a purpose. On the first day of orientation, I saw the stall for the department of Mathematics and Computer Science. I was instantly jealous of the students going there. By the end of the day I was a dual major in Computer Science and Environmental Science. Over the course of the next few years I would try to maintain this dual major status but at every corner I found that my classes conflicted and eventually I made the choice. I had become passionate about computers and programming, and I spent the remainder of my time at WCU focusing on learning everything I could about Computer Science. Since graduating, I've come to realize that this process of learning about computers, programming, and software development never ends - just takes new forms - and I couldn't be more grateful.
